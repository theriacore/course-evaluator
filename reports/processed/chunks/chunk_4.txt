At the international level, the Club of Rome advocated resource conservation for the first time in a more systematic way in 1968. The seminal contribution with respect to resource conservation at the global level was made by the Brundtland Commission Report, 1987. This report introduced the concept of ‘Sustainable Development’ and advocated it as a means for resource conservation, which was subsequently published in a book entitled Our Common Future . Another significant contribution was made at the Earth Summit at Rio de Janeiro, Brazil in 1992.
